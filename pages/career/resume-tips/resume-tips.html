Resume Structure: What Recruiters Look For

Recruiters typically spend only a few seconds scanning a resume. Make sure these sections are clear, relevant and easy to find.

1. Contact Information

Include your full name, professional email, phone number, LinkedIn profile and GitHub/Portfolio links.

2. Career Objective

A short, role-specific summary highlighting your interests, skills and career goals. Keep it concise and tailored to the position.

3. Education

Mention your degree, college, CGPA, graduation year and relevant coursework. This helps recruiters assess your academic background.

4. Technical Skills

List programming languages, frameworks, tools, databases and platforms relevant to the role. Match your skills with the job requirements.

5. Projects

The most important section for freshers. Showcase practical applications of your skills, technologies used and measurable outcomes whenever possible.

6. Certifications & Achievements

Include certifications, hackathons, workshops, awards, competitions and other accomplishments that demonstrate continuous learning and initiative.

Resume Objective Examples

Keep your objective short (2–3 lines), specific to the role, and focused on what you can contribute and learn.

Fresher

Motivated Computer Science undergraduate eager to apply programming, problem-solving and teamwork skills in a professional environment. Seeking opportunities to learn, gain practical experience and build a strong foundation in software development.

Internship

Enthusiastic student seeking a software development internship to apply technical knowledge, collaborate on real-world projects and gain hands-on industry experience while contributing to organizational goals.

Software Developer

Aspiring Software Developer with knowledge of Java, Python and Data Structures & Algorithms. Looking for opportunities to build scalable applications, enhance coding skills and contribute to innovative software solutions.

Web Developer

Enthusiastic developer with experience in HTML, CSS, JavaScript and modern web technologies. Seeking an internship where I can create responsive user interfaces and improve user experiences through practical development projects.

Tip: Customize your objective for each application. Highlight relevant skills, career goals and how you can contribute to the role.

Projects Section Guide

Weak Example

Built a weather app using React.

Strong Example

Developed a weather forecasting web application using React and OpenWeather API, improving user experience through real-time weather updates and responsive design.

Project Description Formula

Write project descriptions using:

Action + Technology + Impact

  • Action → What you did
  • Technology → Tools or frameworks used
  • Impact → Result, improvement, or purpose

ATS-Friendly Tips

Standard Headings

Use Education, Skills, Projects and Experience sections.

Keyword Optimization

Match relevant skills and keywords from the job description.

Simple Formatting

Use clean layouts without unnecessary design elements.

PDF Submission

Export your final resume as PDF before applying.

Action Verbs

Use words like Developed, Designed and Implemented.

Avoid Graphics

ATS systems may fail to parse complex visuals.

Common Resume Mistakes & Recruiter Red Flags

❌ Spelling & Grammar Errors

Always proofread your resume before submitting.

❌ Generic Objective

Customize your objective for every role.

❌ No Projects

Recruiters expect practical evidence of your skills.

❌ Unprofessional Email

Use a professional email address containing your name.

❌ Missing GitHub or Portfolio

Showcase your work whenever possible.

❌ Overly Long Resume

Freshers should generally keep resumes to one page.
