Heuristics and Pathfinding Efficiency

A* Search uses a heuristic (Manhattan distance to target) to prioritize paths that look closer, drastically reducing the search space. Dijkstra's Algorithm expands radially in all directions (uninformed search), guaranteeing the shortest path but exploring more nodes. BFS is unweighted and searches layer-by-layer, while DFS explores deeply along single branches (unsuitable for shortest paths).
