Lock-Free Playground

Multiple threads. One shared structure. Zero mutexes.

Lock-free data structures use Compare-And-Swap (CAS) instead of mutexes — "if the value is still what I read, swap it atomically; otherwise retry." This eliminates deadlock, reduces contention, and powers Java's ConcurrentLinkedQueue and Rust's crossbeam. Step through thread interleavings manually to trigger races, watch CAS succeed or fail, and see the ABA bug happen in real time.
