🔍 How LSH Works

✂️

Step 1: Shingling

Convert each document into a set of overlapping k-grams (shingles). "the cat sat" → {"the cat", "cat sat"}. Two documents with similar content share many shingles, giving a high Jaccard similarity: |A∩B| / |A∪B|.

🎲

Step 2: MinHash Signatures

Apply k random hash functions to every shingle. For each hash function, record the minimum hash value across all shingles. The resulting length-k vector is the MinHash signature — a compact sketch that approximates Jaccard similarity.

🎸

Step 3: Banding

Split each k-dimensional signature into b bands of r rows each. Hash each band. Two documents that share a band's hash become "candidate pairs." Adjusting b and r controls the similarity threshold — more bands catches lower-similarity matches.

✅

Step 4: Verify Candidates

Only compute exact Jaccard similarity for candidate pairs — not all pairs. This is the speedup: instead of O(n²) comparisons, you do O(n·k) hashing plus O(candidates) verification. At scale, candidates is orders of magnitude smaller than n².
