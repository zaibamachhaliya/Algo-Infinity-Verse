Theory

Union-Find efficiently tracks elements partitioned into disjoint (non-overlapping) subsets. It answers two queries: Are two elements in the same set? Merge two sets together.

  • Path Compression Flattens the structure during `Find()` by making every node on the path point directly to the root.
  • Union by Rank Always attaches the smaller tree to the root of the larger tree, keeping the depth strictly logarithmic.

Complexity

Time (Amortized) O(α(N))
Space O(N)

α(N) is the Inverse Ackermann function. Thanks to Path Compression and Union by Rank, operations are effectively O(1) in the real world.
